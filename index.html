Cybersecurity Guidelines

Over 90% of cyber attacks start with human error. Educating yourself on common attack vectors is the critical first line of defense.

🎣

Phishing & Social Eng.

Attacks use deceptive emails or messages to trick you into revealing sensitive data or installing malware.

  • Verify sender addresses carefully.
  • Do not click unverified links.
  • Beware of urgent requests.
🔐

Credential Hygiene

Compromised credentials are the leading cause of data breaches. Defend your digital perimeter.

  • Use robust passwords (min. 12+ chars).
  • Never reuse passwords.
  • Require MFA where possible.
🦠

Malware & Ransomware

Malicious software designed to disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorized access to computer systems.

  • Keep OS and applications updated.
  • Avoid pirated software platforms.
  • Maintain regular offline backups.
📡

Secure Connections

Intercepting data across unsecured networks is a common tactic via Man-in-the-Middle attacks.

  • Avoid public Wi-Fi for banking.
  • Look for HTTPS on websites.
  • Use a reputable VPN.
